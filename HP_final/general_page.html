DISCOVER

The birth of SLIM

It was just a casual conversation with a relative of our group member who is a peasant. It was just a casual glimpse of a news about lead poisoning. Maybe at that time, the seed of SLIM was sowed and was born in this world quietly.

Peasant Wang Ping

We called Wang Ping, he said there was a coating factory near his house.He alsofound that there were indeed fewer earthworms in the earth. Though vegetables seemed to grow faster due to the application of fertilizer, they were also easier to be attacked by pests. That’s why he has been suffering from the loss of it. He thought there must be some relations among factory, earth and crops, which inspired us to use earthworms to protect the heavy metal contaminate d farmlands. We do really want to help peasant like Wang Ping reduce the loss.

Mr.Liu -- a journalist

Mr.Liu, a journalist who investigated a village polluted heavily by lead in Chenzhou, Hunan province, a few years ago. He said Chenzhou was famous for its lead-zinc deposit and learned that most of the people affected by lead poisoning were children. There were only 23,000 children, but over half of them were found to have excessive levels of lead in their blood. They had symptoms like weakened immune systems, slow growth and lack of concentration.

Lead was found in earth, water and crops in their surroundings. That means what they ate, drunk and even breathe in has been severely polluted.

Although it’s been 10 years and the environment has been improved, the vegetables are still in pollution. This incident has drawn our great attention, and we want to help them through synthetic biology.

What did we want to do?

When children's health is at risk, when farmers' interests are lost, when food safety is under threat. We hope one day, lead poisoning will be away from children, the interests of peasants will be protected, and the food safety crisis will not appear.

UNDERSTAND

Communication with one health

By chance we knew One Health, introduced by our group members, drawing our attention. Then we had a coffee hour with One Healthers, after that we got a better understanding to our world and our project.

One Health is a collaborative, multisectoral, and transdisciplinary approach—working at the local, regional, national, and global levels—with the goal of achieving optimal health outcomes recognizing the interconnection between people, animals, plants, and their shared environment.

One Health Club NAU is a club organised by students, building the NAU-UCDavis One Health Joint Center with UCDavis. With environmental degradation, the boundary between human society and nature is going to be indistinct, causing a threat to human health. To solve the problem, One Health was born, holding a promise to attain optimal health for people, animals and environment with the effort of multiple disciplines.

We participated in Lunch Time Challenge with one healthers to share the story of SLIM, where we knew how to explore the communities, institutions, or individuals affected by the problems. Most important of all is that we got kind suggestions and help from Dr. Osburn and Christie, which helped to shape SLIM better.

Dr. Joseph Bressler

Doctor Joseph Bressler is an expert in environmental health and blood lead of The Johns Hopkins University.

Why did we want to ask him?

Since Lead does harm to children severely, we wanted to know how it affects children and is there any effective treatment.

What did we know?

He shared the mechanism of lead poisioning where we knew it’s difficult to cure and the best treatment is to prevent. He said, "In children, the toxicokinetics of lead include absorption through the intestine via iron and calcium transporters, transfer to the blood (99% in rbc and 1% in serum), distribution to soft tissues (e.g. brain and kidney), and storage in the bone. The bone lead remains throughout one’s life and might re-enter the blood when there is bone loss during aging. There is a strong relationship between blood and impaired cognitive development at all blood lead levels. There is no blood level thought to be safe. This is very different from other contaminants that do not increase the risk of cancer."

He also said the rate of lead poisoning in America has declined sharply. Polluted soil is not often to see in America. However, old water pipes remain the most important problem. When water is treated with chlorine, the acidity increases leaching of the lead from the pipe, the water compaies are obligated to test and ads a chemical (phosphate) to insolubilize the lead thereby preventing lead from entering the water supply. This is what happened in Flint, MI but the water company did not add phosphate. So your idea to produce phosphate is right.

Inspector Xu Ningyue

Xu Ningyue is an inspector of agricultural products from The Committee on Agriculture

Why did we want to ask her?

The lead in soil can be accumulated in vegetables, then which are eaten by us. So food safety is important to human health. To know more about that, we wanted to ask her.

What did we know?

Ms. Xu said that in her daily work, it’s often to find exceed problem of heavy metals in agricultural products, such as cadmium in paddy and lead in vegetables. The sources of these heavy metals are very extensive, like industrial pollution, pesticide residues and automobile exhaust. The pollution can be divided into two parts-- exogenous pollution and endogenous pollution. The former like pesticide residues and bacteria can be easily eliminated through common wash and cooking, but not the latter. So the only way to prevent it is stop the gathering in vegetables. That is to solve the environmental problem.

Professor Li Zhen

Professor Li Zhen is an expert in soil microorganism from Nanjing Agricultural University.

Why did we want to ask him?

We wanted to know feasibility of our project and the relations between earthworms and microorganism.

What did we know?

Professor Li said，“Many phosphate-solubilizing microorganisms have a strong tolerance to lead and are largely unaffected. Organic binding states or other forms of heavy metals may affect the final formation of insoluble, but not seriously. Pyromorphite is almost the most stable lead bearing mineral. ”Besides，Professor Li solved the problems of soil environment and lead fixation by phosphorous microorganism, which played a great role in the experimental progress in the our team .

Researcher Zhou Peixiang

Zhou Peixiang is a researcher in management of excrements pollution of livestock and feed safety from Animal Health And Safety Center

Why did we want to ask him?

From the conversations with Dr. Osburn, we’ve known it harms animals. So we wanted to know how do it affect animals.

What did we know?

Ms. Zhou was facinated with our idea. She said, in fact, there are plants using earthworms to remove the heavy metals in excrements of livestock and poultry. The excrements contain heavy metals, and earthworms are tolerant to it. Moreover, feed is sometimes found to have heavy metals problem. This has a huge impact on animal productivity, causing a considerable economic loss to stock-breeding. It can also affect human health through the food chain.

Dr. Jorge L. Mazza Rodrigues

Jorge, a professor in the Department of Land, Air and Water Resources, specializes in soil microbiology. His research interests are microbial ecology, tropical forests, land use change, biodiversity, metagenomics, metaproteomic, ecological genomics, lignocellulose conversion, microbial physiology.

Why did we want to ask him?

We wanted to know role of microorganisms interacting with plants, whether they are beneficial or harmful. In addition, we also wanted to know whether microbial diversity is necessary for farmland.

What did we know?

Professor Jorge said，“There are many benefits of microorganisms interacting with plants: microbes help with biocontrol of other microorganisms, promote nitrogen fixation, provide growth through hormones, allow for nutrients to be recycled and stabilize soil preventing erosion.” After reading her paper Amazon forest-to-agriculture conversion alters rhizosphere microbiome composition while functions are kept ，we could know that the functions are still kept though microbial biodiversity loss in Amazon soils. Therefore, we should consider increasing food production while promoting or maintaining diversity of soil.

For Health For Interest

What is For Health and For Earning?

For Health and For Earning is a scheme to let consumers get healthier food and help peasants get more earning. Government officials encourage contractors to buy SLIM earthworms, and peasants work for them. Then, they produce more green food, which more and more consumers will choose for health. More people choose these, more money peasants can get. Next, we did a lot of work on For Health and For Earning to it’s feasibility.

Occasionally, we found the concept of “one health” and participated in “Lunch Time Challenge” with One Health organism. Doctor Osburn and Chris gave us kind help to perfect our program. By solving the environmental problem, we can decrease the disease by ingesting food with lead and protect earthworm sustaining the ecosphere steady state.

An enterprise

Why did we want to interview her?

We wonder if enterprisers could adopt our project to recover unhealthy earth and how much benefits peasants can get from enterprises.

What did we get?

We interviewed Xiao Yuan, an agricultural enterpriser. She said, “We use a mode of “peasants + cooperation+ family farm”. In China, most farmlands are owned by peasants, so it’s really hard to buy all lands to develop planting. Thus, we rent their lands and pay them for working agriculture, that’s a more stable job than before and attracts many peasants cooperating with us. We have helped more than 3,000 people find jobs and paid 5,317 million yuan in wages. I have known about the way of using earthworms to improve soil, but what I’m concerned about most is the cost. As we all know, it’s much more than just the cost of SLIM earthworms. The expenses of labour and techinique and the loss of earthworms all should be consinderd. If your method is cheaper and more effective than others, I’ll adopt yours.”
