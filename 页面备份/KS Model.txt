              <h4>1. Can we control the amount of CⅠ protein?</h4>
              <p>
                The amount of <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;" href="https://2020.igem.org/Team:NAU-CHINA/Design#general_info_ele_4">CⅠ protein</a> is controllable. We can change RBS strength to change mRNA translation rate or add degradation tag to change the protein degradation rate.
              </p>
              <h4>2. How to explain the effectiveness of the kill switch?</h4>
              <p>
                The engineered <i>Bacillus subtilis</i> did not commit suicide in the laboratory and in the intestine of earthworms because CⅠ repressor protein can bind to the specific inhibition binding site of promoter P<sub>CⅠ</sub> to prevent production of switch RNA. The <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;" href="https://2020.igem.org/Team:NAU-CHINA/Design#general_info_ele_4">kill switch</a> cannot be turned on. When they were expelled out of the intestine, CⅠ proteinand Trigger RNA were no longer produced and always degraded. As the amount of CⅠ decreased, more and more free inhibition binding sites were exposed, more and more Switch RNA were produced, which can combine with Trigger RNA to produceMazF, making the engineered bacteria commit suicide.
              </p>
              <h4>3. Which is the best RBS and degradation tag combination of CⅠ protein?</h4>
              <p>
                In the intestine of earthworms, the production of MazF is the key. Due to the possibility of leakage, the production of MazF is not always at zero. We simulated the leakage of MazF in the intestinal environment to find the combination that generated the least MazF leakage. If the combinations were not unique, considering the simplicity of the pathway design, we would choose the one adding the same degradation tag for CⅠ produced both in the laboratory and in the intestine.
              </p>
            </div>

            <!--2. ABSTRACT -->
            <div class="row general_info_element" id="general_info_ele_2">
              <h3>1 Abstract</h3>
              <p>
                To avoid biological pollution，we modified the gene pathway of <i>Bacillus subtilis</i> by introducing kill switch making that the engineered bacteria do not commit suicide in the intestine, but switch on the suicide mode after they were expelled out of the intestine. To verify the feasibility of the kill switch, we built the Kill Switch Model via <b>MATLAB</b>, which combined the degradation rate of CⅠ protein with different strength RBS. After we quantitatively simulated the effect of the kill switch under various combinations,a reasonable one was given. By adding <b>the LVA degradation tag</b> and selecting <b>B0029 RBS</b>, the kill switch can operate effectively.  
              </p>
            </div>

            <div class="row general_info_element" id="general_info_ele_3">
              <h3>2 Background</h3>
              <p>
                The level of MazF was affected by the RBS and the degradation rate of CⅠ protein, so we chose to adjust the two to find the best combination.
              </p>
              <ul>
                <li style="color:#353535;">Laboratory Culture Phase</li>
              </ul>
              <p>Add IPTG to induce the production of enough CⅠ protein, no MazF.</p>
              <ul>
                <li style="color:#353535;">In the Intestine of an Earthworm</li>
              </ul>
              <p>The accumulated CⅠ protein induced in laboratory degraded, and in the absence of oxygen, <i>Bacillus subtilis</i> expressed CⅠ protein and Trigger RNA, maybe MazF because of leakage.
              </p>

                <li style="color:#353535;">External Environment</li>
              </ul>

As the concentration of CⅠ decreased and the number of free inhibition sites increased, more and more Switch RNA were produced, which can combine with Trigger RNA to produce MazF.
              </p>

            <!--4. MODEL HYPOTHESIS-->
            <div class="row general_info_element" id="general_info_ele_4">
              <h3>3 Model Hypothesis</h3>
              <ul>
                <li style="color:#353535;">The degradation rate of mRNA and protein in a certain amount is a constant.</li>
                <li style="color:#353535;">The mRNA is generated at a constant constitutive transcription rate.</li>
                <li style="color:#353535;">The copy number of plasmids is kept as a constant.</li>
                <li style="color:#353535;">Other species such as RNAP polymerases and ribosomes are kept constant as well.</li>
                <li style="color:#353535;">Reactions are at equilibrium, steady state, or quasi-steady state<sup>[1]</sup>.</li>
                <li style="color:#353535;">Each RBS part has a native strength irrespective of the promoter and protein-coding part it can be used with, and the translation rate with same RBS has a linear relationship with the mRNA length<sup>[1]</sup>.
                <li style="color:#353535;">Since the probability of gene mutation is very small, our model did not consider gene mutation.</li>
              </ul>
            </div>

            <!--6.Design-->
            <div class="row general_info_element" id="general_info_ele_6">
              <h3>5 Design</h3>
              <p>
               By regulating the amount of CⅠ protein, we can control the kill switch. In the laboratory, we selected the concentration of IPTG which could produce the most CⅠ protein. After entering the intestine, RBS strength and CⅠ protein degradation rate were adjusted by using our model to achieve the minimum amount of MazF. In addition, the combination should meet the need that the engineered bacteria can commit suicide as soon as possible when they were expelled out of the intestine.
              </p>
              <!--5.1-->
              <h4>5.1 RBS and Degradation Tag</h4>
              <!--5.1.1-->
              <h5>RBS Selection</h5>
              <p>
                RBS sequence, also called SD ( <i>Shine-Dalgarno</i> ) sequence, is a key controlling the initiation of translation and the expression of proteins, so it determines the level of translation, increasing yield of target product <sup>[3]</sup>. We can change the amount of CⅠ protein produced by selecting suitable RBS strength.
              </p>
              <p>
                To select a more suitable RBS, we selected RBS with different strengths for the test, as shown in <b>Table 5.1.1</b>.
              </p>

              <!--5.1.2-->
              <h5>CⅠ Degradation Tags</h5>
              <p>
                The exact rate of protein degradation depends on a number of factors: the concentration of CLPXP and ClpAP protease and SspB medium; the stability of the protein; the km with the protease binds; the temperature, etc. But we can change the rate of degradation by adding tags. The tags can be recognized by the CLPX foldase and forms a complex with the ClpP protease. The last three residues of the tag determine the strength of the interaction with Clpx, thus determining the ultimate protein degradation rate.
              </p>
              <p>
                We chose three tags: LVA, AAV and ASV. Under the LVA tag, the degradation rate of CⅠ reached 0.1733 min<sup>-1 </sup>, and the degradation rate reached 0.0173 min<sup>-1 </sup> and 0.0087 min<sup>-1</sup> due to AAV and ASV tag<sup>[4]</sup>.
              </p>
              <p>
                <i>Bacillus subtilis</i> expressed CⅠ protein both in the laboratory and in the intestine of earthworms. We can control the degradation rate of both stages at the same time, and choose two degradation tags for the two stages. Respectively, we got some different degradation rate combinations, as shown in <b>Table 5.1.2</b>.
              </p>

              <h4>5.2 CⅠ Rules</h4>
              <p>
                Since CⅠ proteins were produced to form dimer and inhibitory site binding, in this section we had formulated rules for such binding, with CⅠ2 representing the dimer CⅠ repressor and O<sub>CⅠ-1</sub> and O<sub>CⅠ-1</sub> representing the two CⅠ suppressor sites<sup>[5]</sup>
              </p>
             <h6> Dimer CⅠ repressor </h6>
              <p>
                $$ CI2_{f}+O_{CⅠ-1f}\rightleftharpoons CI2:O_{CI-1} $$
                $$ CI2_{f}+O_{CⅠ-2f}\rightleftharpoons CI2:O_{CI-2} $$
                $$ CI2:O_{CI-1}+O_{CI-2f}\rightleftharpoons O_{CI-1}:CI2:O_{CI-2}\rightleftharpoons CI2:O_{CI-2}+O_{CI-1f} $$
                $$ CI2_{f}+D\rightleftharpoons CI2:D $$
              </p>
              <h6> Operating area 1 </h6>
              <p>
                $$ CI2_{f}+O_{CI-1f}\rightleftharpoons CⅠ2:O_{CI-1} $$
                $$ CI2:O_{CI-1}+O_{CI-2f}\rightleftharpoons O_{CI-1}:CⅠ2:O_{CI-2}\rightleftharpoons CI2:O_{CI-2}+O_{CI-1f} $$
              </p>
              <h6> Operating area 2 </h6>
              <p>
                $$ CI2_{f}+O_{CI-2f}\rightleftharpoons CI2:O_{CI-2}
 $$
                $$ CI2:O_{CI-1}+O_{CI-2f}\rightleftharpoons O_{CI-1}:CⅠ2:O_{CI-2}\rightleftharpoons CI2:O_{CI-2}+O_{CI-1f} $$
              </p>
              <p><b>Relationship:</b></p>
              <p>
                $$ \left [ CI2\right ]=\left [ CI2_{f}\right ]+\left [ CI2:O_{CI-1}\right ]+\left [ CI2:O_{CI-2}\right ]+\left [ O_{CI-1}:CI2:O_{CI-2}\right ]+\left [ CI2:D\right ] $$
                $$ \left [ O_{CI-1}\right ]=\left [ O_{CI-1_{f}}\right ]+\left [ CI2:O_{CI-1}\right ]+\left [ O_{CI-1}:CⅠ2:O_{CI-2}\right ] $$
                $$ \left [ O_{CI-2}\right ]=\left [ O_{CI-2_{f}}\right ]+\left [ CI2:O_{CI-2}\right ]+\left [ O_{CI-1}:CⅠ2:O_{CI-2}\right ] $$
              </p>
              <p><b>Where:</b></p>
              <p>
                $$ \left [ CI2:O_{CI-1}\right ]=K_{CIo1}\cdot \left [ CI2_{f}\right ]\cdot \left [ O_{CI-1_{f}}\right ] $$
                $$ \left [ CI2:O_{CI-2}\right ]=K_{CIo2}\cdot \left [ CI2_{f}\right ]\cdot \left [ O_{CI-2_{f}}\right ] $$
                $$ \left [ O_{CI-1}:CⅠ2:O_{CI-2}\right ]=K_{CIo2}\cdot \lambda _{12}\left [ CI2:O_{CI-1}\right ]\cdot \left [ O_{CI-2_{f}}\right ]=K_{CIo1}\cdot\left [ CI2:O_{CI-2}\right ]\cdot \left [ O_{CI-1_{f}}\right ] $$
                $$ \left [ CI2；D\right ]=K_{CId}\cdot \left [ CI2_{f}\right ]\cdot \left [ D_{f}\right ] $$
                $$ \left [ D\right ]=\left [ CI2:D\right ]+\left [ D_{f}\right ] $$
              </p>
              <h6 style="text-align:left;color:#353535;line-height:1.5em;">Note：CⅠ2 represents the dimer CⅠ repressor; O<sub>CⅠ-1</sub> and O<sub>CⅠ-2</sub> represent the two CⅠ inhibition site; A<sub>f</sub> representsA is free; A:B means A binds to B inhibition site; B:A:C means B and C inhibitionsites are both occupied; K<sub>CⅠo..</sub> is the equilibriumassociation constant between the repressor and sites; λ<sub>12</sub> means the ratio of site 1 by DNA looping of repressorand site 2 complex; D means other binding sites;[·] means concentration.</h6>

              <h4>6.1 In the Intestine</h4>
              <p>
                Constitutive expression, not affected by time, place, or environment, has no spatiotemporal specificity. The gene that encodes the protein is not dependent on any transcription factor, so it will continuously transcribe mRNA molecules, and then the translation of the protein will be out of control<sup>[3]</sup>. The expression of CⅠ protein in the intestine of earthworm can be easily described in <b>Figure 6.1.</b> The process of generating Switch RNA and Trigger RNA was similar to the process of producing mRNA<sub>CⅠ</sub> in the figure.
              </p>
              <a href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/a/ad/T--NAU-CHINA--Model_KS_6.1.png" onclick="return false;"><img src="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/a/ad/T--NAU-CHINA--Model_KS_6.1.png" data-toggle="modal" data-target="#Larger_img_modal">
              </a>
              <h6>Fig.6.1. Production and Inhibition Effect of CⅠ Protein</h6>

              <p>
                The CⅠ protein, Switch RNA and Trigger RNA expression can be simply translated into the following biochemical reactions,
              </p>
              <p>
                $$DNA_{CI}\overset{K_{mRNA-CI}}{\rightarrow} mRNA_{CI}$$
                $$mRNA_{CI}\overset{K_{CI}}{\rightarrow}CⅠ$$
                $$mRNA_{CI}\overset{K_{mRNA-CI}}{\rightarrow}\phi$$
                $$CI\overset{d_{CI}}{\rightarrow}\phi$$
                $$DNA_{TR}\overset{K_{TR}}{\rightarrow} Trigger RNA$$
                $$Trigger\quad RNA\overset{d_{TR}}{\rightarrow}\phi$$
                $$DNA_{TS}\overset{K_{TS}}{\rightarrow} Toehold\quad Switch$$
                $$Toehold\quad Switch\overset{d_{TS}}{\rightarrow}\phi$$
              </p>
              <!--6.1.1-->
              <h4>6.1.1 CⅠ Protein Production</h4>
              <p>
                Here, we used <b>ordinary differential equation</b> to calculate the concentration of each substance. The concentration of  mRNA<sub>CⅠ</sub> can be expressed as follows:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\frac{\mathrm{d}\left[mRNA_{CI}\right]}{\mathrm{d}t}={V_{mRNA-CI}}-\left [ mRNA_{CI}\right ]d_{mRNA-CI}$$
              </p>
              <p>
                Where, d<sub>mRNA-CⅠ</sub>(0.1386min<sup>-1</sup>) is the degradation rate of mRNA<sub>CⅠ</sub> and V<sub>mRNA-CⅠ</sub> is the generation rate of mRNA<sub>CⅠ</sub>, which can be expressed as follows:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$  V_{mRNA-CI}= K_{mRNA-CI}\left [ G_{CI}\right ] $$
              </p>
              <p>
                Where, K<sub>mRNA-CⅠ</sub> is the transcription rate, and K<sub>mRNA-CⅠ</sub> = 5.39 min <sup>-1</sup>, [G<sub>CⅠ</sub>] is the copy number of plasmids.
              </p>
              <p>
                Here's what follows:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\frac{\mathrm{d}\left [ CI\right ]}{\mathrm{d}t}=V_{CI}-\left [ CI-2\right ]d_{CI-2}-\left [ CI-1\right ]d_{CI-1}$$
              </p>
              <p>
                  Here, d<sub>CⅠ-1</sub> is the laboratory-induced degradation rate of CⅠ protein, d<sub>CⅠ-2</sub> is the degradation rate of CⅠ protein produced in earthworm intestines, both of which can be adjusted, and V<sub>CⅠ</sub> is the production rate:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$V_{CI}=K_{CI-2}\left [ mRNA_{CI-2}\right ]$$
              </p>
              <p>
                K<sub>CⅠ-2</sub>=4.84 min <sup>-1</sup> is the translation rate of CⅠ generated in the intestine, which could be changed by adjusting RBS. The quantities of CⅠ-1 and CⅠ-2 in the intestine are given by <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;" href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/f/fa/T--NAU-CHINA--model-CⅠ-innerfunction.txt">model_CⅠ_inner_function</a>. The total amount of CⅠ in intestine is shown in <b>Figure 6.1.1.</b>
              </p>

              <h4>6.1.2 Trigger RNA Production</h4>
              <p>
                Similarly, the concentration of Trigger RNA in the intestine can be calculated:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\frac{d\left [ TR\right ]}{dt}=V_{TR}-\left [ TR\right ]d_{TR}$$
              </p>
              <p>
                Where d<sub>TR</sub> is constant, d<sub>TR</sub> = 0.0365 min<sup>-1</sup>, and V<sub>TR</sub> is generation rate of mRNA<sub>TR</sub>, given by the following formula:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$V_{TR}=K_{TR}\left [ G_{TR}\right ]$$
              </p>
              <p>
                K<sub>TR</sub>=19.12min<sup>-1</sup>, [G<sub>CⅠ</sub>] =500 is the copy number of plasmids.
              </p>
              <p>
                The amount of Trigger RNA in the intestine is shown in <b>Figure 6.1.2.</b>
              </p>
              <a href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/7/72/T--NAU-CHINA--Model_KS_6.1.2.jpg" onclick="return false;"><img style="width:75%;" src="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/7/72/T--NAU-CHINA--Model_KS_6.1.2.jpg" data-toggle="modal" data-target="#Larger_img_modal"></a>
              <h6>Fig.6.1.2. The Amount of Trigger RNA in the Intestine</h6>

              <h4>6.1.3 Switch RNA Production</h4>
              <p>
                In order to solve the concentration problem of Switch RNA, we also assumed:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\frac{d\left [ TS\right ]}{dt}=V_{TS}-\left [ TS\right ]d_{TS}$$
              </p>
              <p>
                Where d<sub>TS</sub>is constant, d<sub>TS</sub>= 0.18 min <sup>-1</sup>and V<sub>TS</sub> is generation rate of Switch RNA，given by the following formula:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$V_{TS}=K_{TS}\varepsilon _{1}\left [ G_{TS}\right ]$$
              </p>
              <p>
                K<sub>TS</sub>= 2.52 min <sup>-1</sup>, [G<sub>CⅠ</sub>] =500, ε<sub>1</sub> indicates the binding of CⅠ and inhibition sites, which can be expressed as follows and calculated by <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;" href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/0/0d/T--NAU-CHINA--model-calculate_1.txt">model_calculate_1</a>: 
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\varepsilon _{1}=\left ( \frac{\left [ O_{CI-1f}\right ]}{\left [ O_{CI-1}\right ]}\right )\left ( \frac{\left [ O_{CI-2f}\right ]}{\left [ O_{CI-2}\right ]}\right )$$
              </p>
              <p>
                The amount of Switch RNA in the intestine is shown in <b>Figure 6.1.3</b>, which can be calculated by <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;"href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/d/dc/T--NAU-CHINA--model-tohold-function.txt">model_Toehold_function</a>.
              </p>

              <h4>6.1.4 MazF Production</h4>
              <p>
                The expression of MazF in the intestine of earthworm can be easily described in <b>Figure 6.1.4.1.</b>
              </p>
              <a href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/6/68/T--NAU-CHINA--Model_KS_6.1.4.1.jpg" onclick="return false;"><img style="width:75%;" src="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/6/68/T--NAU-CHINA--Model_KS_6.1.4.1.jpg" data-toggle="modal" data-target="#Larger_img_modal"></a>
              <h6>Fig.6.1.4.1. The Production of MazF</h6>
              <p>
                The specific process of generating MazF is represented by the following formula:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\left [trRNA \right ]+\left [ tsRNA\right ] \overset{K_{1}}{\rightleftharpoons} \left [ tr.tsRNA\right ]$$
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\left [ tr.tsRNA\right ]\overset{K_{tr.ts}}{\rightarrow} \left [ mazF\right ]$$
              </p>
              <p>
                Among them, K<sub>tr.ts</sub> = 2.09min <sup>-1</sup> is the translation rate of generating MazF. K<sub>1</sub> = 1*10 <sup>5</sup>，is the reaction equilibrium constant described as follows:
              </p>
              <p>
                $$K_{1}=\frac{\left [ tr.tsRNA\right ]}{\left [ trRNA\right ]\left [ tsRNA\right ]}$$
              </p>
              <p>
                The total amount of Trigger RNA and Switch RNA are shown as follows:
              <p>
                $$\left [ T_{tr}\right ]=\left [ trRNA\right ]+\left [ tr.tsRNA\right ]$$
              </p>
              <p>
                $$\left [ T_{ts}\right ]=\left [ tsRNA\right ]+\left [ tr.tsRNA\right]$$
              </p>
              <p>We built two functions, <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;" href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/1/14/T--NAU-CHINA--model-calculate_2.txt">model_calculate_2</a> and <a style="color:#f2a8cb;padding-right:0px;" href="https://2020.igem.org/wiki/images/2/26/T--NAU-CHINA--model-maZF-function.txt">model_MazF_function</a>, to calculate the quantities of tr.tsRNA and MazF.</p>
